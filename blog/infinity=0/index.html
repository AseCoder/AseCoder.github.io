Proof that Infinity = 0

Consider the infinite sums of all even and all odd positive integers.

\[\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 2n=2+4+6+8+\dots = \infty\] \[\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (2n-1)=1+3+5+7+\dots = \infty\]

Then, we can add these together and get the sum of all positive integers.

\[\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}2n+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(2n-1)=1+2+3+4+\dots=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n=\infty\]

Simplifying the equation a bit:

\[\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}2n+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(2n-1)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n\]

Then, substituting for the sums:

\[\infty+\infty=\infty\]

At this point it is crucial that we forget that some infinities are bigger than others. Let's transform the equation.

\[\infty+\infty-\infty=0\] \[\infty=0\] \[\tag*{$\Box$}\]

Please do not take this seriously.
