Finnish parliamentary election explained

Also known as d'Hondt's method.

Take a representative candidate, any candidate.

The reference number (\(R\)) for the representative candidate depends on their votes (\(V_r\)) and their party's votes (\(V_p\)).

If \(n\) parties form an election coalition, the coalition's total votes (\(V_c\)) is the sum of the parties' votes.

\[V_c=\sum_{x=1}^n V_{p,x}\]

All of the party's or coalition's \(N\) candidates are ordered by their votes in descending order, creating a list where \[a_i=V_r\] where \(1\le i\le N\). Then, \[R=\frac{1}{i}\cdot V_p\]

(In case of a party coalition, the inverse of \(i\) is multiplied by the coalition's votes, \(V_c\)).

The 200 people with the highest reference number (\(R\)) are elected as representatives in the parliament.
