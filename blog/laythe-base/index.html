My sea base on Laythe

On the 13th of June 2021, I wanted to build a big rocket in KSP. I concluded that a surface base was heavy enough to jusitfy building a big rocket, and I had always wanted to visit Laythe more, so I got to work building a sea base and a launcher for it. The following afternoon I had finished building and testing my rocket, so it was time for the final countdown.

My big rocket after takeoff from Kerbin. 43 Vector engines produce barely enough thrust to get this thing off the ground.

After circularising my low Kerbin orbit, I started my transter burn to the Joolian system where Laythe is.

My rocket's transfer stage has 16 nuclear engines which barely provide a TWR of 0.3

I did my transfer burn in two burns. This picture was taken at apogee after the first burn and features Kerbin and The Mun.

In deep space I made a mid-course correction burn which enhanced my Jool encounter and placed me on a near-equatorial fly-by of Laythe.

I arrived at Jool. Yo mama so fat, Jool could fit inside her. Laythe is also visible in the background.
